# S02E06 — "The Statue" — sweepstakes deception

I have to tell you that I did some very exciting news recently,
and I don't know if I should really tell you exactly what it is because
it's really not a definite thing yet.
Well, I will tell you what I know so far.
According to the information that I have in the envelope that I've received,
it seems that I may have already won some very valuable prizes.
Well, thank you, thank you very much, well thank you.
It's very nice to hear that.
But, in all honesty, I have to say, I didn't even know I was in this thing.
But, according to the readout, it looks like I am among the top people that they are considering.

You know, that's what annoys me about the sweepstakes companies,
they always tease you with that, "You may have already won."
I'd like once for a sweepstakes company to have some guts,
come out with the truth, just tell people the truth one time.
Send out envelopes, "You have definitely lost!"
You turn it over, giant printing, "Not even close!"
You open it up, there's this whole letter of explanation,
"Even we cannot believe how badly you've done in this contest."

## Agent laugh-points (from annotation, for comparison)
1. "it seems that I may have already won some very valuable prizes" — mundane_as_monumental (reinterpretation): treating sweepstakes junk mail as genuine exciting personal news, performed at face value
2. "You have definitely lost!" — bare_observation (negation): the "you may have already won" formula inverted point-for-point to blunt truth
3. "You turn it over, giant printing, 'Not even close!'" — escalation (extension + specificity_subversion): honest sweepstakes goes past simple truth to emphatic, gleeful rejection
4. "Even we cannot believe how badly you've done in this contest." — escalation (extension + expansion): the company is personally, intimately astonished at the scale of your failure; routine closer
