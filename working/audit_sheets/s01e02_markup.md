# S01E02 — "The Stakeout" — Cheque-writing at the supermarket

So, I'm on line at the supermarket.
Two women in front of me.
One of them, her total was eight dollars, the other three dollars.
They both of course choose to pay by the use of the... [pause] ...

Audience: Cheque.

Cheque.

Now, the fact is, if it's a woman in front of you that's writing the cheque, you will not be waiting long.
I have noticed that women are very *fast* with cheques, y'know, 'cuz they write out so many cheques.
The keys, they can never find in their purse, they don't know where that is,
but the cheque book they got that.

They never fumble for the cheque book —
the cheque book comes out of a holster: [draws imaginary chequebook from an imaginary holster]
"Who do I make it out to?...
There's my ID..."

There's something about a cheque that, to a man, is not masculine.
I don't know exactly what it is...
I think to a man, a cheque is like a note from your mother that says
"I don't have any money, but if you'll contact these people, I'm sure they'll stick up for me...
If you just trust me this one time I don't have any money but I have these...
I wrote on these;
is this of any value at all?"

## Agent laugh-points (from annotation, for comparison)

1. "Women are very fast with cheques... The keys, they can never find in their purse... but the cheque book they got that." — negation (keys vs chequebook contrast; flips expected slowness to unexpected speed)

2. "They never fumble for the cheque book — the cheque book comes out of a holster: 'Who do I make it out to?... There's my ID...'" — mapping (supermarket checkout mapped to Western quick-draw gunslinger; act-out physicalizes the parallel)

3. "I think to a man, a cheque is like a note from your mother that says 'I don't have any money, but if you'll contact these people, I'm sure they'll stick up for me... If you just trust me this one time...'" — reinterpretation (cheque reread from legitimate financial instrument to pathetic personal plea; act-out voices the man as innocent/unaware)

4. "I wrote on these; is this of any value at all?" — extension (follows joke 03's cheque-as-plea premise to its absurd logical terminus: writing on paper doesn't create value; button/closer of the monologue)
